Rural Flood Awareness Tips

1. Build on Higher Ground
If possible, choose elevated areas for farming, livestock, and homes to reduce flood exposure.
2. Construct Flood Channels
Simple ditches and channels can help guide rainwater away from homes and crops.
3. Raise Livestock Pens
Ensure animals are kept in elevated shelters to avoid injury or drowning during floods.
4. Use Traditional Knowledge
Incorporate indigenous practices like early flood signs, seasonal planting shifts, and communal alerts.
5. Store Crops Securely
Keep harvests in waterproof containers or raised granaries to avoid spoilage.
6. Plant Flood-Resistant Crops
Choose varieties that can tolerate excess water if you farm in lowland areas.
7. Community Awareness Sessions
Gather villagers to share tips and warnings when heavy rains are expected.
