Flood-Prone Area Awareness Tips

1. Know Your Local Risk
Check if your area is officially marked as flood-prone by local authorities or environmental agencies.
2. Avoid Building in Low-Lying Areas
These areas are more likely to flood, especially during heavy rain or overflowing rivers.
3. Keep Drainage Paths Clear
Blocked stormwater drains increase the chances of local flooding. Help by clearing rubbish from ditches, gutters, and drains.
4. Build with Flood-Resistant Materials
Use water-resistant bricks, cement, and design elements that withstand prolonged water exposure.
5. Create an Emergency Plan
Know where to go, who to contact, and what to do in case a flood hits your area. Keep emergency numbers handy.
6. Stay Informed During Rainy Seasons
Use weather apps, radio alerts, or follow official social media channels for updates.
7. Raise Household Items
Store electronics and important documents above ground level, especially in known flood-prone homes.
8. Install Water Barriers or Sandbags
Place these at doors, driveways, or any low entry points when a flood warning is issued.
9. Keep a Flood Emergency Kit Ready
Include water, food, flashlight, batteries, power banks, first-aid items, and waterproof storage for documents.
